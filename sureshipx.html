SureShipX

AI-Powered Logistics & Fulfillment Platform

Role Founder · Product Owner · UX Designer
Timeline 2024
Type Logistics · E-Commerce · AI
Stack React · Node.js · Python · SQL · Tailwind CSS · APIs

Overview

SureShipX is a logistics and fulfillment platform used by e-commerce brands to manage shipments, tracking, and operations across markets. It integrates with couriers and ERPs to automate status updates, tracking notifications, and operational workflows — giving merchants a single source of truth for their entire logistics operation.

As Founder and Product Owner, I was responsible for the product vision, the UX of every customer-facing and internal interface, and the collaboration with engineers to bring it all to life.

The Problem

E-commerce merchants juggle multiple courier integrations, inconsistent tracking updates, and fragmented tools that make it nearly impossible to maintain visibility over their shipments. The result: frustrated customers flooding support channels with "Where is my order?" queries, operational blind spots, and missed SLA commitments.

  • Merchants lacked a unified view of shipments across multiple courier partners
  • Tracking updates were delayed or inconsistent, causing customer anxiety
  • Support teams were overwhelmed with WISMO (Where Is My Order) tickets
  • Logistics teams had no efficient way to handle exceptions and errors at scale

Research & Discovery

I conducted in-depth interviews with e-commerce merchants, logistics managers, and support teams to understand the end-to-end fulfillment experience. Key findings shaped the product direction:

  • Merchants needed real-time shipment visibility with clear exception handling
  • Customer-facing tracking pages needed to be branded and informative to reduce support load
  • Logistics operators needed scan-based workflows that minimize manual data entry
  • The biggest pain point was not knowing about problems until the customer complained

UX Process

Information Architecture & User Flows

I mapped out the workflows for three primary user types: merchants managing orders, logistics operators processing shipments, and end customers tracking packages. Each flow was designed to surface the most critical information first and reduce clicks to common actions.

Wireframing & Iteration

I designed wireframes for the shipment tracking dashboard, order management views, customer tracking pages, and operational scan-based flows. Each was iterated based on merchant feedback and operational constraints discovered during pilot testing.

Key UX & Product Contributions

  • Shipment tracking dashboard with clear status indicators and exception flags
  • Order management view with filters, bulk actions, and SLA indicators
  • Customer tracking page designs that reduce support queries by providing proactive updates
  • Operational workflows for logistics teams including scan-based flows and error handling

Final Designs

The production designs deliver a clean, data-dense interface that balances information density with clarity. The merchant dashboard surfaces shipment status at a glance, while drill-down views provide the detail needed for exception handling. The customer-facing tracking page was designed to be branded, mobile-first, and informative enough to preempt support tickets.

Key Learnings

Building SureShipX taught me that in logistics, the most impactful UX work often happens in the exception paths — not the happy paths. When a shipment is on track, the interface barely needs to exist. But when something goes wrong, the design needs to surface the problem, explain it clearly, and make resolution effortless.

I also learned the importance of designing for operational speed. Warehouse teams and logistics operators don't have time for complex interfaces — every extra click costs real money at scale. This drove me to design scan-based flows and keyboard-first interactions that minimize friction.
