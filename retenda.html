Retenda

AI-Driven Product Engagement & Retention Tool

Role Product Manager & UX Lead
Timeline 2024
Type SaaS · AI · Retention
Stack React · Node.js · Tailwind CSS · Figma · ChatGPT-4o

Overview

Retenda is a SaaS tool that helps digital products increase engagement and retention using AI-driven reminders, journeys, and feedback loops. It gives product teams a clear view of what's working, what's not, and what to do next — all within a simple, actionable interface.

As Product Manager and UX Lead, I defined the feature set, created user personas and journeys, and designed the end-to-end experience from onboarding through daily use.

The Problem

Most digital products struggle with retention — they acquire users but can't keep them engaged. Product teams often lack visibility into why users churn and don't have an easy way to set up targeted re-engagement campaigns. Existing tools are either too complex (requiring dedicated analytics teams) or too simplistic (basic push notifications with no intelligence).

  • Product teams couldn't easily identify at-risk users or understand churn patterns
  • Setting up retention campaigns required technical expertise and multiple tools
  • No clear connection between retention actions and measurable outcomes
  • Existing solutions were either enterprise-complex or consumer-simplistic

Research & Discovery

I conducted interviews with product managers, growth leads, and founders at early-stage SaaS companies to understand how they currently approach retention. Key insights included:

  • Teams wanted a "retention dashboard" that shows cohort performance at a glance
  • Most were doing retention work manually — spreadsheets, ad-hoc emails, gut-feel decisions
  • The ideal tool would suggest what to do, not just show data
  • Onboarding needed to be fast — teams wanted to see value within the first session

Personas

I created three primary personas: the Growth PM who needs cohort analytics and campaign tools, the Founder who needs a quick overview of retention health, and the Marketing Lead who wants to schedule targeted reminders and campaigns.

UX Process

Onboarding Design

I designed an onboarding flow that configures retention journeys in just a few steps — connect your data source, define your key retention events, and Retenda starts building your first retention dashboard automatically. The goal was to deliver an "aha moment" within 5 minutes.

Dashboard & Insights

The main dashboard shows retention metrics, cohort performance, and AI-generated suggestions in a single view. I designed the information hierarchy so that the most actionable insights are always visible above the fold, with drill-down capabilities for deeper analysis.

Campaign & Reminder Builder

I designed interfaces for scheduling reminders and campaigns with clear previews — so teams can see exactly what their users will receive before it goes live. The builder supports conditional logic and timing rules while keeping the UI approachable for non-technical users.

Key UX Contributions

  • Quick-start onboarding that configures retention journeys in a few guided steps
  • Retention dashboard showing cohort performance, trends, and AI-driven suggestions
  • Campaign builder with preview functionality and conditional scheduling
  • Insights page connecting retention actions to measurable outcomes
  • Responsive design ensuring the tool works across desktop and tablet for on-the-go teams

Outcomes

Clearer Strategy

Improved clarity of retention strategy for early customers, giving teams a structured approach to engagement

Faster Insights

Helped teams quickly see what's working and what's not in their engagement efforts

Actionable Loops

Connected retention data to concrete actions, closing the loop between insight and intervention

Key Learnings

Retenda taught me that the best analytics tools don't just show data — they tell you what to do with it. Every screen I designed had to answer two questions: "What's happening?" and "What should I do about it?" This principle shaped the entire product experience.

I also learned the value of progressive disclosure in complex tools. By starting users with a simple dashboard and only revealing advanced features as they become relevant, we dramatically reduced the learning curve and increased activation rates during early testing.
